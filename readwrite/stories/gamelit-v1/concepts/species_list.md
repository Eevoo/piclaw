Playable Species — initial candidates

1) Human (Amahara-born)
- Daily life: craftspeople, merchants, shrine attendants, versatile.
- Party roles: balanced (skill flexibility), social capital gains, guild-bound.
- Notes: common; social nuance and family/clan ties matter.

2) Kawa-kin (riverfolk with subtle amphibious traits)
- Daily life: live along canals, skilled fishermen, excellent swimmers, specialized in watercraft.
- Party roles: scouts, boat-handlers, stealth in water, ceremonial purifiers.
- Notes: stigmatized in some guilds; unique cultural rites involving tides.

3) Tsukime (moon-eyed folk)
- Daily life: nocturnal markets, scholars of arcane rites tied to lunar cycles.
- Party roles: magic-adjacent support, ritual specialists, knowledge-holders.
- Notes: often recruited by Shrine Orders; rare and respected.

4) Kijitsu (clock-blooded)
- Daily life: integrated with clockwork prosthetics, many are artisans or laborers in steam districts.
- Party roles: engineers, tech-specialists, heavy-hitters with mechanical augmentations.
- Notes: a social class tension exists between fully organic and augmented individuals.

5) Hanabira (petal-tanned forest folk)
- Daily life: live near island groves, herbalists, gardeners for ceremonial plants.
- Party roles: healers, foragers, environmental interaction specialists.
- Notes: deeply tied to seasonal festivals and shrine offerings.

Species Culture Notes
- Marriage, apprenticeship, and guild membership customs vary by species.
- Some species have racial bonuses (e.g., Kawa-kin swim speed, Kijitsu mechanical affinity) but social penalties may apply.
- Encourage roleplay: species differences affect how NPCs react and what daily tasks look like.
