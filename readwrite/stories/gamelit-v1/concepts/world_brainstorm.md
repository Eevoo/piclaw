World Brainstorm — gamelit-v1

Tone and Style
- A game-lit / lit-RPG tone filtered through poetic descriptions of everyday life.
- Emphasis on rituals, social hierarchy, work, and the small mechanics of living in a Japanese-inspired fantasy city.
- Occasional sharp steampunk contrast: brass, gears, pneumatic whistles, steam trams, and clockwork artisans.
- Focus on character growth, group dynamics, and repeated episodic challenges (quests, tournaments, guild tasks).

Setting Outline
- Geography: Island-archipelago nation called Amahara — a string of city-islands connected by skybridges and steam ferries.
- Cities blend wooden shrine architecture, narrow lantern-lit alleys, and industrial districts with ironwork factories.
- Daily life dominated by guilds and clans; local shrines and market districts are social hubs.

Game Lit Mechanics (in-world)
- "The Reckoning" — an in-world competitive system that ranks adventurers by achievements, contracts fulfilled, and social capital.
- Players (called 'seekers') register with a Guild ID and have a handheld "Kairo" device — a steampunk talisman that records quests, stats, and permits interface with arcane machinery.
- Progression combines reputation (social), practical skill ranks, and in-game currency (shiden).
- Win condition: unclear at start — multiple possible endgames (rise to guildmaster, open a new island, ascend to the Celestial Registry, overturn the Reckoning). The player chooses a path by decisions.

Factions and Power Centers
- Shrine Orders: custodians of ritual magic and registry rites. Conservative, influential in social rank.
- Guild Houses: mercantile and adventurer networks that control contracts and training.
- Clockwrights: engineers and tinkerers who build Kairo devices and steamwork — a rising power with sometimes-dubious ethics.
- Merchant Clans: wealth and trade, manage ferries and skybridges.
- The Magistrate: city government with strict codes and taxes; has its own enforcement arm.

Everyday Life Details
- Morning ritual: tea, incense, register at the local shrine for health/skill bonuses.
- Street vendors selling fried lotus, candied starfruit, and small clockwork trinkets.
- Apprenticeships common; craftspeople train within guild houses for decades.
- Seasonal festivals offer special Reckoning events and rare rewards.

Potential Hooks for Episodes
- A missing petition that alters the player's guild rank.
- A street contest where clockwork pets race and reveal cheating rings.
- A shrine theft that forces unusual collaboration between Shrine Orders and Clockwrights.
- A ferry accident that reveals a smuggling operation.

Notes on Atmosphere
- Blend cozy daily scenes (tea, work, camaraderie) with the mechanical hum of steam-era tech.
- Keep the "game" systems present but allow human-scale storytelling: small personal stakes often matter more than leaderboards.
