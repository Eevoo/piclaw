# Birdfolk

## Basic Information

**Playable**: Yes
**Average Natural Lifespan**: 50 years (maturity around age 15, elders at 40)

## Narrative Description

Birdfolk are strikingly elegant humanoids shaped by the sky. With feathered bodies, powerful winged arms, and taloned feet, they move with a grace that belies their strength. Their hollow bones and keen senses make them natural explorers, scouts, and messengers—always watching, always listening, always ready to take flight. Though they may seem aloof or distant, birdfolk are deeply connected to the rhythms of nature and the wind, and they carry themselves with a quiet confidence born of knowing they can rise above any challenge. They are often seen as enigmatic, their sharp beaks and piercing eyes giving them a watchful, almost ancient air. Yet beneath their aloof demeanor lies a deep curiosity and a love of beauty, whether in song, sky, or the smallest detail of a landscape.

## Physical Description

Birdfolk are bipedal humanoids covered with feathers, sporting talons for feet and powerful winged arms adapted for flight. Rather than true hands, birdfolk make do with large primary feathers at the end of their wings—quite dextrous, but nowhere near as strong or precise as the hands of most other species. This wing-hands trade-off is the central tension of the species: extraordinary mobility at the cost of fine dexterity.

Their feet are equipped with sharp talons, ideal for perching, climbing, and defense. Feathers cover their entire bodies, ranging in color and pattern from subtle to dazzling, often reflecting their environment and overall health.

Their beaks are strong and precise, used for eating, grooming, and even delicate tasks like crafting. Hollow bones reduce their weight, enabling flight, but also make them more vulnerable to injury. Birdfolk are even lighter than catfolk—this extreme lightweight frame is what enables flight but creates significant fragility.

### Morphology

Standing between 4'6" and 7'2", birdfolk are slender and lightweight, with a height-to-weight ratio that favors flight. Their bodies are covered in soft feathers that shift color in sunlight. Their eyes are large and forward-facing, giving them excellent depth perception. They have a long, feathered tail that helps with balance and steering in flight. Their faces are beaked, with no visible ears—instead, they have small, hidden auditory openings. Males and females are similar in appearance, though males often have more vibrant plumage during mating season.

### Skeletal System

Birdfolk have a lightweight, hollow-boned skeleton adapted for flight. The bones are porous and filled with air sacs, reducing mass while maintaining strength. This structure makes them more fragile than humans in direct impact, but also allows for greater agility and energy efficiency.

### Digestive System

Birdfolk are omnivorous, with a digestive system adapted to process high-protein, high-energy foods. They consume seeds, fruits, small animals, and insects, often hunting or foraging while airborne. Their beaks are used to tear and crush food, and they have a specialized gizzard to grind tough materials.

### Senses

Their most dominant sense is sight. Birdfolk have exceptional visual acuity—up to 10 times sharper than humans—allowing them to spot prey or danger from miles away. Their hearing is acute, though not as refined as their vision. They can detect subtle changes in air pressure and wind patterns, giving them an innate sense of weather and air movement. They lack a sense of smell, but compensate with hyper-awareness of sound and motion. Birdfolk also report being able to see what they call navigation lines in the sky, bands that appear to point north and south; they can perceive these bands even when their eyes are shut.

Birdfolk are also capable of **Songspeak**—ultrasonic communication frequencies beyond the range of most species' hearing. This ability is shared with catfolk and androids, allowing these three species a private channel of communication when they choose to use it.

## Culture & Habits

Birdfolk value freedom, precision, and beauty. They are often solitary or live in small, close-knit flocks, preferring to keep to the skies and avoid large, ground-bound settlements. Their society is built on observation, communication, and mastery of flight. They are natural storytellers, using complex calls, wing gestures, and feather displays to convey emotion and meaning. Many birdfolk are artists, musicians, or navigators, and they hold a deep reverence for the wind, the sun, and the stars.

Birdfolk are well suited for vertical environments and thrive in places where flight is an advantage, such as the rooftops of urban districts or the peaks of Ridgeback County. However, even for birdfolk, the extreme heights and density of cities like Atlas Central, or the sheer cliffs of Ridgeback County, can be daunting. Many birdfolk find the heights of airships and antigrav flying cars even more stressful than natural heights—there is a difference between trusting your own wings and trusting a machine. In regions like Teatown, the combination of fog and verticality presents unique challenges, but birdfolk adapt with their natural flight and social flocking behaviors.

### Domestic Life

Birdfolk live in elevated nests built into cliffs, treetops, floating platforms over water, or sky islands. These nests are often intricate, woven from feathers, vines, and bone, though younger generations have been known to simply purchase lumber. They bathe in high-altitude streams or rain, and preen their feathers at least daily with their beaks to maintain flight readiness, lest they get itchy. They wear minimal clothing, favoring lightweight, aerodynamic garments made from silk, feathers, or woven plant fibers. They are self-sufficient, using their wings to carry tools and supplies, and they often trade songs, stories, or rare feathers as currency amongst each other, whilst carrying heavy human currencies like silver are reserved for contact with ground-striders.

### Martial Life

In combat, birdfolk rely on speed, precision, and aerial dominance. They use their talons and beaks as weapons, and their wings can deliver powerful strikes or be used to shield or knock back foes. They are often deployed as scouts, assassins, or snipers, using the sky to outmaneuver and surprise enemies. Their ability to hover and change direction mid-air makes them difficult to hit.

The birdfolk employ two primary tactics when fighting ground-dwelling opponents:

* They may use nets to capture enemies, then carry them high into the sky before dropping them, allowing gravity to deliver the final blow.
* Alternatively, they wield bows and arrows with their talons, enabling them to fly and hover while aiming and firing—maintaining aerial superiority during combat.

### Interaction with Other Cultures

#### Humans

Humans often view birdfolk as mysterious, even magical. They are fascinated by their ability to fly and often see them as messengers of the gods. Some humans fear them, believing they spy on the world from above. Others seek to ally with them, hoping to gain access to their knowledge of the skies.

#### Other Species

Elf and gnome communities often admire birdfolk for their grace and connection to nature. Dwarves and orcs tend to distrust them, seeing them as unpredictable and detached. Catfolk respect their keen senses and agility, and sometimes form alliances with them. Half-catfolk and birdfolk often form close bonds, sharing a love of observation and movement.

## Gameplay Mechanics

**Flight**: Natural flight capability; birdfolk excel at scouting and aerial observation. They see Brassmoor from above, serving as natural messengers and scouts when conditions allow.

**Wing-Hands Trade-off**: Primary feathers at wing-ends provide limited manipulation instead of true hands. This is the species' defining tension—unmatched mobility at the cost of dexterity.

**Extreme Lightweight**: Even lighter than catfolk, enabling flight but creating significant fragility.

**Environmental Vulnerabilities**:
- Strong winds require battening down or risk being blown around
- Heightened sensitivity to toxic gases
- Nearly impossible to swim

**Aerial Perspective**: Birdfolk perceive the world from a vantage point no other grounded species can match—rooftops, spires, and open sky are their domain.

**Songspeak**: Ultrasonic communication frequencies shared with catfolk and androids.

## Mechanical Description

### Characteristics

* Brawn: 1
* Agility: 3
* Intellect: 2
* Cunning: 2
* Willpower: 2
* Presence: 2

### Derived Attributes

* Wound Threshold: 8 + Brawn
* Strain Threshold: 10 + Willpower
* Soak: 2 + Brawn
* Silhouette: 1
* Encumbrance Threshold: 3 + Brawn (while flying), 5 + Brawn (on the ground)

### Species Starting Bonuses

* Starting Experience: 65 xp
* Starting Skills: Eagle Eyes - Birdfolk begin with one rank in Perception. They may not train Perception above rank 2 during character creation.
* Special Abilities:
  * **Flyer**: Birdfolk can fly and hover by flapping their wings, provided they are below their flying encumbrance threshold (3 + Brawn).
  * **Hollow Bones**: Birdfolk automatically add +10 to any critical injury result they receive, due to their flight-adapted bodies being less able to absorb impact than a human’s.
