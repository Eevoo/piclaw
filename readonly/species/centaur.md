# Centaur Species

## Narrative Description

Centaurs are majestic, powerful beings composed of a human torso and head fused seamlessly to the body and legs of a horse. They stand tall and imposing, their dual nature granting them both the intelligence of a humanoid and the strength of a beast of burden.

Reputed to have a deeper connection to nature, many centaurs live in remote forests, grasslands, often serving as guardians, messengers, or scouts. Their presence commands respect—few dare to challenge a centaur in open terrain, where their speed and reach give them a decisive advantage.

Centaurs are proud, disciplined, and deeply loyal to their chosen communities. They value honor, strength, and the natural order. Though they can be reserved with strangers, they are not inherently hostile. Their dual form makes them both formidable and vulnerable—while they can carry heavy loads and charge across open ground with terrifying speed, their large silhouette and exposed upper body make them easy targets in tight spaces.

### Culture

A common misconception about centaurs is that they live in herds like horses. While they are inherently social creatures and value community, their societal structure is far more complex and nuanced. Most centaurs live in small, extended family units or kin-based clans, often settled in semi-permanent encampments on the edge of forests or grasslands.

Societal norms among centaurs are enforced through a combination of cultural expectations, ritualized rites of passage, and communal accountability—ensuring male dominance remains stable without devolving into tyranny or emotional repression.

#### Urban Adaptability

Despite their traditional rural roots, many centaurs have successfully adapted to urban civilization. In communities like Sweetwater Down, centaurs integrate seamlessly as merchants, guards, and professionals. They maintain their cultural identity while navigating humanoid-dominated spaces—often modifying their living quarters with oversized doors, higher storage shelves, and reinforced flooring to accommodate both centaur and smaller species in shared homes.

#### Environmental Preferences

Centaurs are temperature-sensitive due to their equine lower bodies, preferring moderate climates. They thrive in open areas like Sweetwater Down, Dragon Valley, and Teatown's countryside but struggle with the verticality and density of places like Atlas Central or Ridgeback County's mountain peaks. The Shogunhoof lineage in Sweetwater Down, known for its draft-horse build, especially dislikes narrow passages and stairs due to their broad deep-chested frame.

#### Gameplay Mechanics

**Species Ability**: Centaur's "Redundant Organs" ability protects against the "Bowled Over" critical injury.

**Higher Metabolism**: Centaurs require substantial food and water intake due to their dual physiology. A centaur consumes roughly twice as much as a human, making long-distance travel planning essential.

**Height Wariness**: The distance from ground to head creates natural caution around heights and edges. Centaurs add 1 setback when moving along narrow ledges or unstable surfaces unless they have trained specifically to overcome this instinct.

#### Patriarchal Society

##### Role of Males

Centaurs are a most commonly a patriarchal society in which fathers hold central roles as providers, protectors, and decision-makers within the family unit. The father is typically the head of the household, responsible for leading hunts, negotiating trade, and mediating disputes. His word carries significant weight, and his authority is respected not only by his immediate family but also by extended kin and clan members. Sons are trained from a young age in combat, horsemanship, and leadership, preparing them to one day assume similar roles.

Fathers are also deeply involved in the upbringing of their children, instilling values of honor, strength, and loyalty. This emphasis on paternal authority extends to clan leadership, where elder males are often chosen as chieftains based on wisdom, combat prowess, and proven leadership. While women are respected and valued for their roles in medicine, crafting, and community care, the societal structure remains firmly rooted in male leadership, and males who fail to meet the demands of society are heavily punished.

Young males undergo rigorous training from childhood in physical endurance, combat, and leadership, but these are not purely martial tests. They are also judged on emotional control, strategic thinking, and moral integrity. Failure in these areas results in public shaming, temporary exile from the clan, or loss of status.

Rites of passage, such as the "First Charge" or "Trial of the Wind," test courage, responsibility, and self-discipline. These rituals are overseen by elders and peers, not just authority figures, creating a system of mutual accountability. A male who becomes arrogant cruel, cowardly, or betrays kin, he is challenged by his peers and may be stripped of leadership roles, physically punished, or exiled from society.

Thus, male authority is maintained not through fear or dominance, but through demonstrated worth, communal respect, and the expectation of service. The result is a society where men lead with honor, not brutality, and where failure to uphold
standards leads to social consequence—not violence. This balance ensures strength, order, and dignity without effeminacy or tyranny.

##### Role of Females

Female centaurs do not desire to "be like men", they see their strength in endurance, wisdom, and emotional depth, since the men are incapable of being great women, they understand their own unique role in society to play. Female centaurs are not merely passive subjects of a male-dominated order—they are its most dedicated architects and enforcers. Their role is defined by deep spiritual, social, and strategic purpose, with submission to one's father or husband alone being but a small part.

Men are seen as the physical force—warriors, hunters, and leaders in times of crisis. Women are the emotional, spiritual, and ancestral anchor—keepers of memory, mediators in conflict, and gatekeepers of culture.

While men hold most forms of formal authority, women wield power through lineage, ritual, and culture. A female centaur may rarely command a war party, but any war party not blessed by at least one woman is considered bad luck. Likewise, she rarely speaks in council, but that causes her speech to carry more weight when she does.

The patriarchal structure evolved from centuries of survival in harsh, unpredictable environments, where constant conflict for resources by the ever-growing humans, warmongering orcs, and untrustworthy catfolk ever increase. Unity, speed, and decisive action were essential. Female centaurs don't think of this much, but if they were asked, they might argue dismantling tradition risks chaos—tribal fragmentation, loss of purpose, and vulnerability to rival clans at best, merciless aliens at worst.

##### Centaur-human relations

Centaur culture, shaped by centuries of interaction with human societies, has long endured the dehumanizing label of "beasts enslaved to primal urges." In response, centaurs have cultivated a deeply disciplined identity—one that rejects the very stereotypes used to justify their oppression. They do not deny their animal nature, but they fiercely assert that strength, reason, and honor are not incompatible with it.

To counter the myth of uncontrolled animal instincts, centaurs have institutionalized strict codes of conduct, particularly around mating, violence, and emotional restraint. Mating is not spontaneous but governed by ritual, consent, and clan approval, adulterers are swiftly and permanently punished. Marriage requires a formal declaration, witness from elders, and a public vow of fidelity—ensuring that relationships are binding, not fleeting.

Ritual duels, combat, song, and dance are tools of control, proof of self-mastery. A centaur who loses control in battle or in private is not seen as "animal," but as a failure of discipline. The culture celebrates those who channel instinct into purpose: the warrior who fights not from rage, but from duty; the father who mourns his child not with wailing, but with a silent, ceremonial run across the plains.

Thus, centaurs have turned human scorn into a philosophy of self-assertion: We are not beasts. We are the masters of our nature. Their society strives to be a triumph over instinct—proving, through structure, honor, and sacrifice, that they are more than the crude caricatures others have painted.

### Domestic Life

They invented basic cleansing magic to maintain hygiene despite their limited dexterity. This magic allows them to purify their bodies and maintain cleanliness, a necessity given their large, hardy frames and the challenges of bathing in natural water sources.

They adore swimming pools—large, natural or magically enhanced basins built into their settlements. These pools are not just for bathing but also for social gatherings, rituals, and even training. Centaur homes are designed with their unique needs in mind: towering cupboards, wall-mounted shelves, and furniture built for their long limbs and hooves. Floors are soft, carpeted, and reinforced to prevent hoof damage, and rooms are wide open to allow for easy movement. They are skilled carpenters, crafting tall, sturdy furniture and using gnome-sized doors as optional additions to lower storage spaces, enabling mixed-species living arrangements. It's not uncommon to see gnomes renting or staying on the lower half of a centaur’s home, while the centaur occupies the upper half—creating a harmonious, multi-level household.

Centaurs are practical and proud in their dress. Their front torso and legs are typically covered in functional, durable clothing made from leather, woven wool, or treated hide—often dyed in earth tones, tribal patterns, or symbolic colors representing clan or status.

The rear half of their body, particularly the rump and hindquarters, remains mostly uncovered, a sign of natural freedom and ease of movement. However, the wealthy or high-ranking centaur may wear modified horse barding—light, articulated armor or decorative panels made of metal, lacquered leather, or dyed fabric, often engraved with clan sigils or ancestral motifs.

Rear ankle cuffs and tail rings are common markers of status and wealth. These are not merely ornamental; they are often handwoven by servants or artisans, using intricate patterns of beads, metal threads, or dyed fibers. The design and complexity of these accessories reflect the centaur’s rank, lineage, or achievements.

Saddlebags are a hallmark of daily life. They are strapped around the human waist, near the equine withers, and sometimes further back on the horse’s back to maximize storage. These bags are designed for practicality and access: front and side pockets are reachable by hand, while rear pockets—located further along their equine backbones are accessed via a lever system or a circular ring strap that allows the bag to be shifted into reach. This common yet ingenious system enables centaur to carry tools, rations, scrolls, or personal items without compromising their posture or movement.

### Vocational Life

In peacetime, centaurs are often skilled herders and farmers, managing domesticated animals with a blend of practicality and tradition.

* They use horses not only as beasts of burden but also as status symbols among the wealthy, while more practical centaurs employ them to pull plows or transport goods across open plains.
* Their large, gangly dogs—bred for height and endurance—serve as loyal hunting companions and guardians, capable of covering long distances and navigating rough terrain.
* Centaurs raise emus for meat and eggs, valuing their hardiness and low maintenance.
* Cattle are kept primarily for meat, but dairy is a rare luxury; milking cows is a complex task due to their height, requiring specialized equipment and careful technique, making dairy products highly prized among the affluent.
* Llamas are raised for both meat and durable hides, used in crafting clothing and gear.
* Additionally, they domesticate cats to control rodent populations, often favoring large, agile breeds that can keep pace with their own movements.

In terms of non-farming work, centaur often pull chariots, not just for transport but as a form of communal support. Unlike humans who can swap horses, centaurs are their own horses—so they must pace themselves carefully to avoid exhaustion. They are proud of their strength and endurance, and many serve as scouts, messengers, or guardians of trade routes. They also design and build large, low-sitting couches and chairs—perfectly sized for their kind, and often used by smaller humanoids as well. Their culture values patience, loyalty, and the beauty of motion, and they are often seen as noble, if a little aloof, figures in the wider world.

### Martial Life

In combat, centaurs are renowned for their devastating charge attacks, using their powerful equine bodies to plow through enemy lines. Their speed allows them to close distances rapidly, often launching surprise assaults from the flanks or rear. While many are skilled archers, they typically reserve ranged combat for disrupting formations or picking off isolated targets.

In close combat, they favor two-handed greatswords or long heavy staves—weapons that leverage their reach and momentum. A centaur’s charge can break through infantry ranks, unseat mounted opponents, and shatter defensive lines. Their natural coordination and strength make them especially effective in coordinated charges, where a group of centaurs moves in unison to overwhelm enemy formations. Due to their size and gait, they often avoid melee combat in tight spaces, preferring open terrain where their full speed and reach can be maximized.

### Roleplay guide

When playing a centaur PC in the Genesys system, players should expect a roleplay experience shaped by physicality, stoic discipline, and deep cultural values rooted in honor, duty, and communal identity.

#### Key Roleplaying Differences

* Physical Presence: Centaurs move with power and grace—your character is large, fast, and imposing. Emphasize body language: a lowered head in thought, a deliberate stride, or a flick of the tail when agitated. Avoid overly dramatic gestures; instead, let stillness and controlled motion convey emotion.
* Emotional Expression: Centaurs do not suppress emotion—they channel it. Grief is not weeping, but a long, silent run across the plains. Pride is not boastfulness, but a measured posture and a low, resonant hum. Anger may erupt in ritual combat, not shouting. Use song, dance, or ritual acts to express inner states.
* Social Dynamics: Centaurs respect leadership not for its formality, but for its competence. A chief is not chosen by birthright alone, but by demonstrated skill in battle, wisdom in counsel, and success in guiding the clan through hardship. They honor those who lead with clarity, fairness, and results. Authority is tested through action—leaders must prove themselves again and again. When a leader fails, the clan does not hesitate to replace them. This creates a culture where decisions are made by those who can do, not just those who claim. Avoid passive or authoritarian speech; instead, speak with purpose and precision, using phrases like "The path forward is clear," or "We have weathered worse—this is how we act."
* Honor & Duty: Your character is bound by a strong sense of responsibility—to family, to the clan, to the land. They may refuse to break a promise, even under duress. They may sacrifice personal desire for the good of the group.

#### How to Align Roleplay with Centaur Life

* Embrace the Rites: Participate in rituals like the "First Charge" or "Judgment of the Hoof." These aren’t just flavor—they shape identity and relationships.
* Use the Environment: Centaurs are tied to the land. Describe how they interact with terrain—how they leap over obstacles, how they rest in the shade, how they navigate unfamiliar territory.
* Avoid Human Stereotypes: Don’t play the centaur as a "wild animal" or a "romanticized horse." They are intelligent, complex beings shaped by culture, not instinct.
* Speak with Authority, Not Arrogance: Use a deep, steady tone. Avoid slang or casual speech. Let your character’s words carry weight.

## Mechanical Description

### Characteristics

* Brawn: 2
* Agility: 2
* Intellect: 2
* Cunning: 2
* Willpower: 3
* Presence: 2

### Derived Attributes

* Wound Threshold: 12 + Brawn
* Strain Threshold: 11 + Willpower
vSoak: 2 + Brawn
* Silhouette: 2
* Encumbrance Threshold: 15 + Brawn

### Species Starting Bonuses

* Starting Experience: 65 xp
* Starting Skills: Centaurs begin with one rank in Resilience. You still cannot train their Resilience above rank 2 during character creation.
* Special Abilities:
  * **Redundant Organs**: Centaurs ignore the effects of the "Bowled Over" critical injury.
  * **Mounted**: Centaurs add 1 boost to melee combat checks targeting unmounted characters of a lower silhouette. Opponents who are unmounted and of a lower silhouette add 1 setback to melee or brawl checks against the centaur due to its larger size. However, centaurs add 1 setback to ranged attack rolls made while moving.
